Building a Dola Agent is a straightforward process that takes you from a blank canvas to a live, purchasable product on the marketplace. This guide walks you through each step in order — from naming your Agent to publishing it for buyers. Follow these steps the first time you create an Agent, and the process will feel natural every time after.

## Step-by-Step: Create and Publish Your Agent

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Creator Dashboard and start a new Agent">
    Go to [dolaio.cn](https://dolaio.cn) and sign in to your creator account. From the Creator Dashboard, click **New Agent**. This opens the Agent editor where you'll complete all configuration before publishing.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set a name and upload an avatar">
    Give your Agent a clear, memorable name that reflects its purpose. A name like "Legal Brief Assistant" or "Python Tutor" tells buyers exactly what they're getting before they even read the description.

    Upload an avatar image to give your Agent a visual identity. Avatars appear on the marketplace listing, in the buyer's workspace, and in every conversation thread.

    <Note>
      Use a square image at least 256×256 px for the best display quality across all surfaces where your Agent appears.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Write a short public description">
    The public description is your Agent's storefront. It appears on the marketplace listing and is the first thing a potential buyer reads. Keep it concise — two to four sentences — and focus on what the Agent does, who it's for, and what problem it solves.

    <Tip>
      Include specific example use cases in your public description to help buyers immediately understand what your Agent does. For example: "Use this Agent to draft reply emails, summarize long threads, and suggest follow-up actions — all from a single prompt."
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Write the system prompt and define the Agent's persona">
    The system prompt is the most important part of your Agent. It contains the detailed instructions that define how your Agent behaves, what it knows, what it avoids, and how it communicates. Buyers expect your Agent to work correctly from the very first message — and the system prompt is what makes that possible.

    A strong system prompt covers:
    - **Role and purpose** — What is this Agent? What is its job?
    - **Tone and communication style** — Formal or casual? Concise or detailed?
    - **Knowledge scope** — What topics does it handle? What should it decline?
    - **Response format** — Does it use bullet points, markdown, short answers, structured output?
    - **Edge cases** — How should it handle ambiguous or off-topic requests?

    <Tip>
      Write a clear, detailed system prompt. Buyers expect the Agent to work right out of the box. A vague or sparse system prompt leads to inconsistent responses and poor reviews — invest time here before you publish.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select an AI model">
    Choose the AI model that will power your Agent's reasoning and responses. Different models have different strengths — some excel at conversation and reasoning, others at code, and others at creative tasks.

    Open the **Model** dropdown in the Agent editor and select the model that best fits your Agent's purpose. See the [Models page](/agents/models) for guidance on matching models to use cases.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add skills (optional)">
    Skills are modular capabilities that extend what your Agent can do beyond standard conversation. You can add skills from the **Skills** tab in the Agent editor. Examples include web search, image generation, file handling, and external service integrations.

    Adding skills is optional, but Agents with relevant skills consistently deliver more value — and higher-value Agents sell better.

    See the [Skills page](/agents/skills) for a full breakdown of available skill categories and how to add them.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set your price">
    Choose how buyers pay for your Agent. Dola supports two pricing models:

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="One-Time Purchase">
        Buyers pay once and own access to your Agent permanently. This model works well for Agents that deliver a specific, contained type of value — a specialized writing assistant, a niche research tool, or a focused task automation.
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Subscription">
        Buyers pay a recurring fee — monthly or annually — for continued access. This model works well for Agents that buyers will return to regularly, such as a daily productivity assistant or an ongoing customer support tool.
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>

    <Note>
      Dola covers initial traffic costs for buyers who try your Agent. This means new buyers can experience your Agent before they've fully committed to a purchase, which lowers the barrier to buying and increases your conversion rate.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Preview your Agent and test responses">
    Before publishing, use the built-in preview to test your Agent. Send it sample messages that represent real buyer scenarios. Check that:

    - The Agent responds according to your system prompt
    - The tone and format match what you described in your public description
    - Edge cases and off-topic requests are handled gracefully
    - Skills (if added) activate correctly in context

    Iterate on your system prompt until the Agent consistently behaves the way you intend.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Publish to go live">
    When you're satisfied with your Agent's configuration and test results, click **Publish**. Your Agent will appear on the Dola marketplace immediately, visible to all buyers browsing in your Agent's category.

    After publishing, you can return to the Agent editor at any time to update the name, avatar, description, system prompt, model, skills, or pricing. Changes take effect immediately for all existing and future buyers.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## After You Publish

Publishing is not the end — it's the beginning. Keep an eye on buyer feedback and conversation patterns to understand how people actually use your Agent. Use that information to refine your system prompt, add new skills, or adjust your pricing. The most successful creators on Dola treat their Agents as living products that improve over time.
