The AI model you choose for your Agent is one of the most consequential decisions you'll make as a creator. It determines the quality of reasoning, the tone of responses, the types of tasks your Agent can handle, and how buyers experience it from the very first message. Dola gives you the freedom to choose from leading models across major AI providers, and you can change your model selection at any time without disrupting your buyers.

## Model Freedom on Dola

Dola does not lock you into a single AI provider or model tier. When you create or edit an Agent, you choose the model that powers it from a curated selection of top-tier models from major providers. This means you can match the right model to your Agent's specific purpose — rather than forcing every Agent through a one-size-fits-all default.

<Note>
  Specific model availability may vary over time as providers release new models and retire older ones. Check the **Model** dropdown in your Agent editor for the current list of available models.
</Note>

## How to Select a Model

You can choose or change your Agent's model at any point during creation or after publishing:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Agent editor">
    From your Creator Dashboard on [dolaio.cn](https://dolaio.cn), click on the Agent you want to configure — or click **New Agent** if you're starting fresh.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to the Model dropdown">
    In the Agent editor, locate the **Model** section. Click the dropdown to see the full list of available models.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your model">
    Choose the model that best fits your Agent's purpose. The dropdown displays key characteristics for each model to help you decide.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your changes">
    Click **Save** (or **Publish** if this is a new Agent). The selected model becomes active immediately for all conversations — including for existing buyers if you're updating a published Agent.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Choosing the Right Model for Your Use Case

Different models have different strengths. Use the guidance below to match a model category to your Agent's primary purpose.

<CardGroup cols={1}>
  <Card title="Conversational and Reasoning Agents" icon="comments">
    If your Agent's primary job is to have nuanced, context-aware conversations — answering questions, advising on decisions, explaining complex topics, or providing customer support — choose a model known for strong reasoning and natural language understanding. These models handle long context windows well and maintain coherent multi-turn conversations.

    **Best for:** assistants, advisors, tutors, support agents, research helpers
  </Card>
  <Card title="Image Generation Agents" icon="image">
    If your Agent needs to create, edit, or analyze visual content, choose a vision-capable or image-generation model. Some models can both understand images sent by the user and generate new images in response to prompts.

    **Best for:** creative assistants, design tools, visual content generators, image editing helpers
  </Card>
  <Card title="Coding and Technical Agents" icon="code">
    If your Agent works primarily with code — writing it, reviewing it, debugging it, or explaining it — choose a model that is fine-tuned for technical tasks. These models are trained on large codebases and understand programming languages, frameworks, and software engineering concepts with much greater precision than general-purpose models.

    **Best for:** coding assistants, code reviewers, debugging tools, documentation generators, DevOps helpers
  </Card>
  <Card title="Generalist Agents" icon="sparkles">
    If your Agent handles a wide variety of tasks without a single dominant use case, a high-quality general-purpose model offers the best balance of capability across domains. These models perform well at writing, analysis, question-answering, summarization, and reasoning simultaneously.

    **Best for:** all-purpose productivity assistants, multi-domain research tools, personal AI companions
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Initial Traffic Costs

Dola covers the initial usage costs for buyers who try your Agent after purchasing. This means buyers can interact with your Agent — powered by whichever model you've chosen — without immediately incurring usage charges themselves. Here's how it works:

- **Initial trial period:** Dola absorbs the model inference costs during the buyer's first interactions with your Agent.
- **After the trial:** Once the initial period ends, usage costs are billed based on the plan the buyer is on. Buyers are aware of this before they purchase.

This model is intentional. By removing the financial risk of trying a new Agent, Dola makes buyers more likely to engage deeply with your Agent — which means better experiences, stronger reviews, and higher long-term retention.

## Updating Your Model After Publishing

You are not locked into your original model choice. You can change the model powering your Agent at any time from the **Agent settings** in your Creator Dashboard.

<Note>
  When you update the model on a published Agent, the change takes effect immediately. All existing buyers automatically begin using the updated model — you do not need to notify them or create a new listing. Their experience continues without interruption, just powered by the new model.
</Note>

This flexibility lets you keep your Agent competitive as new and better models become available. Top creators on Dola routinely update their Agents to take advantage of new model releases, which improves the quality of existing buyer experiences and attracts new buyers browsing the marketplace.
