Every listing on Dola has a clear, creator-set price displayed before you commit to a purchase. Whether you're browsing the marketplace as a buyer or publishing your first Agent as a creator, this page explains the available pricing models, how fees work, and practical tips for setting a price that converts.

## For buyers

Dola supports two purchase types, and each listing uses one of them. You'll always see the model and price prominently on the listing page before you check out.

### One-time purchase

Pay once and keep access to the Agent or skill permanently. One-time purchases are ideal for specialized tools, utility Agents, and anything you want to use indefinitely without a recurring commitment.

### Subscription

Pay a recurring fee — monthly or annually — for continuous access to the Agent. Subscriptions are common for Agents that deliver ongoing value, such as daily task automation or regularly updated data feeds. Annual plans, when offered, typically come at a lower effective monthly rate.

### Free trials

Some listings include a free trial period. Look for the **"Try"** badge on the listing page to identify Agents that let you evaluate before you pay. Trial terms are set by the creator and displayed alongside the listing price.

### Supported payment methods

Dola accepts credit and debit cards for all buyers. Depending on your region, additional local payment methods may be available — open checkout to see the full list of options supported in your location.

<Note>
  Prices are displayed in your local currency where Dola supports it, so you always know exactly what you'll pay without having to convert.
</Note>

## For creators

When you publish an Agent or skill, you set the price in the listing editor. Dola gives you full control over the model, the amount, and any promotional terms.

### Choosing a pricing model

In the listing editor, select either **One-time purchase** or **Subscription** as the pricing model for your listing. You cannot mix models on a single listing, but you can publish separate listings for the same Agent under different models if you want to offer buyers a choice.

For subscriptions, you can offer monthly billing, annual billing, or both. If you enable both, buyers choose at checkout.

### Setting your price

Enter your desired price in the listing editor. As you type, Dola shows you a **live preview of your net earnings** after the platform fee is deducted — so you can work backwards from your target take-home amount and adjust accordingly.

### Introductory discounts and promotional periods

You can attach an introductory discount or a limited-time promotional price to any new listing. Promotional pricing is shown clearly to buyers on the listing page and expires automatically at the end date you set. You can also offer a free trial period for subscription listings.

### Pricing tips

<Tip>
  Subscription pricing works best for Agents that deliver ongoing, recurring value — for example, a daily briefing Agent, a live data monitor, or a workflow automation that runs on a schedule. Buyers are comfortable paying monthly when the benefit is continuous.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  One-time pricing is simpler and often converts better for specialized or utility Agents — tools that solve a specific problem once and don't require regular updates or continuous operation.
</Tip>

## Platform fees

Dola charges a percentage fee on each successful sale. Here's what you need to know:

- **Where to find your rate** — the exact fee percentage for your account is displayed in **Creator Dashboard → Listings** alongside the net earnings preview for each listing.
- **Automatic deduction** — Dola deducts the fee before crediting revenue to your balance. You never invoice Dola or remit fees separately.
- **Consistent across models** — the same fee structure applies to both one-time purchases and subscription payments.

Because you see your net earnings in the editor before you publish, there are no surprises after a sale goes through.
